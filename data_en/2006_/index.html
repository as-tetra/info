Ross Paul Taylor

2006.11.28 - 12.10 *closed on Mondays
13:00〜20:00

organised by art space tetra
supported by Arts Council England

The exhibition will showcase works by landscape artist Ross Paul Taylor, Taylor uses both imagined landscapes as well as the materiality of landscape to address our problems of understanding space and ratio within nature.

Taylor’s works often develop using a process of repetition to underline the complexity of understanding the space around him. By repeating the same creative action, Taylor makes only small and often near insignificant steps, until something far grander eventually develops. It is this
persistence with time and process, which Taylor wants the viewer to indulge in, so that the audience is exposed to a struggle significant only to the artist as human being, trying to understand his surroundings.

A talk by Emma Lewis "Our Location Now - new media and our engagement with place"

2006.08.22 (tue)
20:30〜
¥free

Trampoline is a new media art organisation, based in Nottingham and Berlin promoting the innovations of emerging artists on both a local and international level. Emma’s presentation will be concerned with how new media art can come to terms with the struggle between the local and the global.
Trampoline has been particularly examining pervasive and locative media, which are making significant challenges to our concept of location and our interaction with different environments. Emma is concerned with connecting places and facilitating a meaningful dialogue. She will be discussing the works of some of the artists which Trampoline is currently working with including Blast Theory and Active Ingredient and will present some of her personal projects which aim at creative communication between different spaces.

Anthony Guerra

+ Kazuhiko Skaguchi, Kai Lam

2006.08.12 (sat)

¥1,500（include 1drink）

anthony guerra plays guitar and occassionally sings. he is from sydney, australia but currently lives in kawasaki, japan.

he has released over 20 cds, cdrs and cassettes on labels including paradisc (kyoto), absurd, impermanent, pseudo arcana and more.

he also runs the record labels TwoThousandAnd (with Michael Rodgers) and Black Petal.

about anthony's recent solo record "empty kingdoms" on black petal - "devastating... the type of album every musician wants to make. Guerra's music is the kind of stuff you internalize. It sticks to your bones" BRAD ROSE/FOXY DIGITALIS

"sweet, sad poisons.. I like it a lot" BRIAN OLEWNICK / BAGATELLEN

"It's utter desolate music.. Strumming his guitar, lonely, this still has traces of improvisation, but it moves outside. The influence of Loren MazzaCane Connors is never far away in this lonely blues, or that of Richard Youngs when it comes to his singing. Occasionally there are some percussive sounds or a slide guitar, but other that it's music of despair, grief and perhaps hope. It's great stuff, in all it's sadness."
" FRANS DE WAARD / VITAL
